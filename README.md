# Decision Intelligence Portfolio

## About This Work

This portfolio brings together applied projects focused on decision intelligence — the structured use of data to support operational and strategic decision-making.

Rather than focusing on model complexity or technical novelty alone, these projects emphasise:

- Clear problem framing  
- Decision-ready analytics  
- Responsible automation design  
- Executive interpretability  
- Governance awareness  

The objective across all work is consistent:  
transform raw data into structured, explainable decision support systems.

---

## Project 1  
### Customer Growth Analytics Using Decision Intelligence

This project explores how transactional e-commerce data can be transformed into structured customer growth strategies.

The work analyses:

- Revenue trends and seasonality patterns  
- Retention impact on long-term revenue stability  
- Behavioural customer segmentation  
- Automation-ready engagement frameworks  

Rather than stopping at descriptive insight, the project evaluates whether each finding can realistically inform operational automation while maintaining human oversight.

Key Themes:
- Revenue stability through retention
- Segment-driven decision support
- Explainable automation logic
- Responsible marketing intelligence

## Project 2  
### NHS Operational Pressure & Waiting List Decision Framework (RTT 2025)

This project analyses national NHS Referral-to-Treatment (RTT) waiting list data (April–December 2025) to identify structural pressure points across specialties.

The work includes:

- National waiting list trend analysis  
- 18-week and 52-week breach risk profiling  
- Development of a composite Operational Pressure Score  
- Specialty-level hotspot identification  
- Structured escalation framework recommendations  

The aim is not predictive modelling, but operational decision intelligence: enabling structured, evidence-based intervention planning within complex public systems.

Key Themes:
- Public sector decision support
- Operational risk identification
- Structured escalation modelling
- Transparent analytical design

Repository:  
👉 [NHS RTT Decision Framework Project](https://github.com/kamranafridi9220-prog/nhs-rtt-operational-pressure-framework-2025)

## Project 3
### UK Telecom Customer Switching Intelligence

This project analyses customer switching behaviour within the UK telecommunications market using publicly available Ofcom Switching Tracker data.

The analysis focuses on understanding the behavioural drivers behind telecom provider switching and how telecom operators can identify early churn signals.

The project explores:

• Frequency of customer switching across telecom providers  
• Behavioural triggers influencing switching decisions  
• Competitive pressures within the UK telecom market  
• Development of a telecom churn decision framework

Rather than focusing purely on descriptive statistics, the project demonstrates how market-level behavioural data can support proactive customer retention strategies within competitive telecom environments.

Key Themes:

• Telecom churn decision intelligence  
• Behavioural switching signals  
• Retention strategy design  
• Market-level decision support frameworks

## Analytical Philosophy

Across both commercial and public-sector contexts, the approach remains consistent:

1. Define the decision problem clearly  
2. Ensure analytical transparency  
3. Prioritise interpretability over complexity  
4. Evaluate automation suitability before recommendation  
5. Embed governance awareness from the outset  

Decision intelligence is not about replacing human judgement.  
It is about strengthening it.
